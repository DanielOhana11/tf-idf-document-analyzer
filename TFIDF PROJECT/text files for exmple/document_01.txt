Pizza Recipe: A Classic Italian Delight

Pizza is one of the world's most beloved foods, originating from Naples, Italy. Making pizza at home is easier than many people think and requires just a few basic ingredients.

For the dough, you'll need 500 grams of all-purpose flour, 325 ml of warm water, 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 1 tablespoon of sugar, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Begin by dissolving the yeast and sugar in warm water, letting it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy. Mix the flour and salt in a large bowl, then add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.

For the sauce, use crushed tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, salt, oregano, and fresh basil. Simply blend these ingredients together for a quick and flavorful sauce.

Once your dough has risen, divide it into portions and roll each out into a circle. Preheat your oven to its highest setting (usually 250°C/480°F). Spread the sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust. Add your favorite toppings such as mozzarella cheese, pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, or olives.

Bake the pizza for 10-15 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling. The result is a delicious homemade pizza that rivals any pizzeria.
