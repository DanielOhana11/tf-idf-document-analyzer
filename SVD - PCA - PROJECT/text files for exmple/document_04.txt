Workout Routine for Beginners: Start Your Fitness Journey

Starting a fitness journey can feel overwhelming, but a well-structured beginner workout routine makes it manageable and effective. This program focuses on building strength, improving cardiovascular health, and establishing consistent exercise habits.

Week 1-2: Foundation Phase
Begin with three workout days per week, allowing rest days in between for recovery. Each session should last 30-40 minutes. Start with a 5-minute warm-up of light cardio such as walking or cycling, followed by dynamic stretches.

Strength Training: Perform bodyweight exercises including 10 squats, 10 push-ups (modified on knees if needed), 15 lunges per leg, and a 30-second plank. Complete two sets of each exercise with 60-90 seconds rest between sets.

Cardio: Add 15 minutes of moderate-intensity walking, jogging, or cycling. Aim for a pace where you can still hold a conversation but feel your heart rate increase.

Week 3-4: Progression
Increase to four workout days per week. Add an extra set to strength exercises and extend cardio sessions to 20-25 minutes. Introduce new movements like mountain climbers, glute bridges, and wall sits to challenge different muscle groups.

Cool Down: End each session with 5 minutes of static stretching, focusing on major muscle groups. This helps improve flexibility and reduces soreness.

Remember to listen to your body, stay hydrated, and gradually increase intensity to avoid injury and build sustainable fitness habits.
